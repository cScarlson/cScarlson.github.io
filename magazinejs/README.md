
MagazineJS
================
This repository encapsulates demonstrations of common or unique approaches to common or unique problems.

## Background
Oftentimes one may spring for a library because a particular solution has been mystified by higher-ups in the development community. Even worse, one may even spring for an entire framework just because a small piece of the framework solves the issue. The real problem is that we, as developers, have moved away from patterns and allowed a library author to hookwink us by simply encapsulating the pattern within a library and mystifying the solution so much that we believe it is doing more than it really is.

In this Show & Tell repo, we'll look at the following problems & solutions and more. We'll use any combination of frontend languages (HTML, CSS, JavaScript) alongside _slanguages_ such as SCSS, LESS, Markdown, and others. TypeScript may be used in cases where attention to types is especially necessary. We will not aim to reinvent the wheel as, in those cases, a library or framework is usually more justified. However, we may bump up against that line or even move past it as there may be multiple tradeoffs that could encourage one to roll out a solution by hand. The question you, as the developer(s), should always ask: _if something goes wrong, do I want to use my own time to fix the issue?_ On the one hand: I might want to rely on a team of other [say, opensource] people to solve it for me. On the other hand: I might want not be able to afford that luxury when we need the solution _yesterday_. It is a matter of Control vs Responsibility; you can't have one without the other.
